About Basil🌿

Basil🌿 The Programming Language

Why?

As a life-long computer programmer, I've learned a lot about programming languages.

In 1984 I was a freshman in college and took a course called COP-1000. It was the first course taught to students on their journey to becoming a computer programmer. It was a course that taught BASIC.

From that point on I was "hooked" on programming, and became a career coder. Of course I moved on to working with dBase, Quickbasic, and C, then C++ and PowerBASIC, VB and C#, then Java, then Php, Javascript, Frameworks and Web development, Go, Rust, and so on. I've had a long and fulfilling career in programming.

At age 60, I thought it might be interesting to go back to college and take a course in something new but that COP-1000 course caught my attention. What would that same course be like more than 40 years later? How was the trade of computer programming being introduced to new learners so long after the 1980's when everything was exciting and new to me?

In spite of my decades of experience, I had not had much opportunity to learn Python, so that was going to be a net positive too, not to mention the $1000's of dollars in free software licenses I could get with my college student email address ;)

Going to campus was fun, and the professor taught the class well, and we had some delightful chats about our mutually shared experiences as he was actually older than me.

However I noticed that one thing was really absent this time around and that was any kind of real enthusiasm among my fellow students. Many of them withdrew from the course after the first week and the ones who remained were not really interested in the course. Many of them expressed a common goal of learning how to make games and apps and this course did very little to tap into their little sprouting passion for, maybe, becoming a computer programmer.

While Python is a very useful language, and very important for anyone interested in coding to master, it is not the first language that I would recommend to anyone looking to learn computer programming. Nothing about Python seemed to cultivate a deep passion for programming, or spark any of that "magic" the was so inspirational to me and my fellow students in the 1980's.

BASIC was a very different language. It was a language that was very easy to learn, and that was full of potentially powerful features that could be used to make a lot of different things. It was a language that made your wheels turn and ignite the imagination. It was a language that was very fun to learn and use.

Python.. not so much :(

So, I made Basil🌿 The Programming Language. It is intended to be a friendly, modern BASIC that is delightful to learn and use, but powerful enough to be used for any modern programming task. It is designed to teach new coders the basics of programming, to help them become proficient in other languages with it's flexible Basic-like and C-like syntax, and real-world Mods like SQL, AWS, and AI.

"Basil🌿 is a Modern, Mod-able, AI-aware, OO (or not) BASIC language Bytecode Interpreter and Cross-Platform Compiler, written in Rust, with lots of Rad Mods such as AI, AWS, Zip, Crypt (Base64, PGP) CrossTerm, Inet (SMTP, FTP, Json, Curl, REST, etc), SQL(MySQL/RDS, Sqlite, ORM, etc), MIDI (Audio, DAW) and even a Totally Tubular "OK Prompt" CLI mode (Jolt Cola not included)"
